Chérie Class

Terms and Conditions of Enrolment

These Terms and Conditions govern the Chérie Class Programme (the "Programme"), operated by the Martian Embassy, Barcelona Sector, Third Orbit (the "Embassy"), a division of Mexzungu Group Limited. By accruing cosmic miles or accepting any benefit under the Programme, the Candidate (the "Holder") is deemed to have read, understood, and accepted these Terms in their entirety. Ignorance of these Terms is noted and found charming. It does not constitute a defence.

1. Programme Overview

The Chérie Class Programme confers upon the enrolled Holder a designated status entitling her to certain privileges during the Validity Period, subject to the conditions set forth herein. The Programme is administered by Intergalactic Pepe (the "Ambassador"), acting on behalf of the Embassy and under the general supervision of the Committee.

The Programme operates across all sectors recognised by Martian communications protocol. Earth-side geography is acknowledged but does not constrain Programme activity.

2. Eligibility

2.1 Chérie Class status is awarded at the sole and absolute discretion of the Committee. The Committee's selection criteria are proprietary and not subject to disclosure, inquiry, or peer review.

2.2 Status cannot be applied for. It is conferred. Applications submitted through any channel — written, verbal, or telepathic — will be received, filed, and not acted upon.

2.3 Eligibility requires demonstrated accumulation of cosmic miles (defined in Section 7). The precise threshold is determined by the Committee on a case-by-case basis and communicated via the Ambassador at a time of his choosing.

2.4 Status is not available to all candidates. The Embassy makes no representations, warranties, or apologies in this regard.

3. Activation and Validity Period

3.1 The Programme activates upon formal notification by the Ambassador. The Validity Period is thirty (30) Earth days from the Activation Date.

3.2 Activation constitutes acceptance of these Terms in full. The Holder is not required to sign anything. The universe witnesses it.

3.3 Partial benefits apply during the Probationary Period, defined as the interval between Activation and successful completion of the Fourth Experiment. See Section 5.

4. Programme Benefits

During the Validity Period, the Holder is entitled to the following benefits, subject to the conditions herein:

  • (a)
    Right of Address. The right to be addressed as chérie by the Ambassador in all communications, formal and informal, across all channels recognised by Martian protocol. This right is personal, non-transferable, and — when invoked — entirely unreasonable to argue against.
  • (b)
    Priority Access. Priority access to Intergalactic Pepe, subject to orbital schedule and mission availability. The Embassy does not guarantee response times but acknowledges, on the record, that they will be unreasonably fast.
  • (c)
    Co-Pilot Designation. A complimentary co-pilot seat on select missions, as determined by the Ambassador. Co-pilot status does not confer command authority over the mission. Spirited input from the co-pilot seat is, however, actively encouraged and will be taken seriously.
  • (d)
    Experiential Privileges. Access to slow, dangerously comfortable experiences. The nature, duration, and itinerary of such experiences remain at the Ambassador's discretion. The Embassy accepts no liability for side effects including but not limited to: unexpected contentment, difficulty returning to orbit, and permanently recalibrated expectations.
  • (e)
    Cosmic Miles Accrual. The Holder shall continue to accrue cosmic miles throughout the Validity Period at the prevailing rate, as determined by the Committee. Miles accrue automatically and do not require the Holder to do anything in particular. That is, in fact, partly how they accrue.

5. Conditions of Full Enrolment

5.1 Full enrolment and access to the complete benefit set is conditional upon successful completion of the Fourth Experiment (the "Threshold Condition"). The nature and parameters of the Fourth Experiment are known to the Ambassador and shall be communicated in due course, through appropriate channels, at a pace the Committee considers correct.

5.2 Prior to satisfaction of the Threshold Condition, the Holder shall have access to a defined subset of Programme benefits as determined by the Committee (the "Probationary Period"). The Holder is encouraged to assess which benefits are active during this period empirically. The Committee considers this part of the experience.

5.3 Satisfaction of the Threshold Condition is acknowledged by the Ambassador and confirmed in the Committee's records. No certificate is issued. The Holder will know.

6. Limitations and Restrictions

6.1 Singularity Clause. Chérie Class status is awarded to one (1) Holder at a time. The Programme does not operate on a pooled, shared, or concurrent basis. The Embassy takes this clause seriously and expects the Holder to do the same.

6.2 Non-Transferability. Status, benefits, accrued cosmic miles, and the right of address are personal to the Holder and may not be transferred, assigned, gifted, sublet, borrowed, inherited, or exported to any third party in any galaxy.

6.3 Committee Discretion. The Embassy reserves the right to suspend, modify, or revoke benefits at any time without prior notice, stated reason, or apology. This right exists. It is exercised rarely. Its existence is nonetheless load-bearing.

6.4 The Programme is not combinable with other loyalty schemes, competing orbital arrangements, or alternative diplomatic frameworks operating in the same sector.

7. Cosmic Miles

7.1 "Cosmic miles" refers to the proprietary metric developed by the Embassy to measure frequency, resonance, orbital alignment, and the quality of attention paid to the texture of things. The full methodology is not publicly disclosed. It is, however, accurate.

7.2 Cosmic miles are earned through proximity, presence, and the particular kind of effort that does not announce itself. They are not earned through compliance, performance, or applications submitted through official channels.

7.3 Miles do not expire within the Validity Period. Accumulated miles inform Committee decisions on renewal. The Committee is always watching the balance.

8. Renewal

8.1 Chérie Class status does not auto-renew. At the conclusion of the Validity Period, the Embassy initiates a formal Committee review.

8.2 Renewal criteria include, without limitation: cosmic miles accumulated during the Validity Period, atmospheric compatibility, completion of the Threshold Condition, trajectory, and — the Committee will admit — a factor that does not reduce to any formula yet devised.

8.3 The Committee does not guarantee renewal. The Committee does not guarantee anything. The Committee observes, deliberates, and decides. Its decisions are not subject to lobbying, though the Holder is permitted to continue being herself, which the Committee finds more persuasive than lobbying in any case.

8.4 The Holder will be notified of the renewal decision via the Ambassador, through the usual channels.

9. Termination

Status terminates automatically, without notice, upon the occurrence of any of the following:

  • Breach of Protocol. Any act materially inconsistent with the standards of the Programme, as determined by the Committee in its sole judgement. The Committee will know it when it sees it.
  • Insufficient Cosmic Frequency. A sustained reduction in orbital resonance below the threshold required to maintain active status. Resonance is not measured in messages per day. The Committee uses different instruments.
  • Failure to Board. If the boarding door is open and the Holder does not board, the seat is considered vacated. The Embassy does not hold the door. The Ambassador will note this in the file. The file is permanent.
  • Force Majeure of a Non-Cosmic Nature. Events outside both parties' orbital jurisdiction, subject to the Committee's classification of what qualifies. Classifications are final.

10. Governing Law and Disputes

10.1 This Programme and all benefits, obligations, interpretations, and entanglements arising hereunder are governed exclusively by Martian Law, as interpreted by the Embassy, Barcelona Sector, Third Orbit.

10.2 All disputes, questions, grievances, or requests for clarification shall be submitted to the Committee. The Committee's decision is final, binding, issued without appeal, and delivered at a time the Committee deems appropriate, which may not be immediately.

10.3 Earth-side legal frameworks — including but not limited to the laws of France, Spain, the European Union, and the United Kingdom — are noted and, with due respect, not applicable. The Embassy extends this position to all other jurisdictions in the known solar system.

11. Amendments

The Embassy reserves the right to amend these Terms at any time. Amendments are issued by the Ambassador and take effect upon issuance. The Holder is deemed to have accepted all amendments by continuing to accrue cosmic miles, which the Committee considers the most meaningful form of consent.

12. Entire Agreement

These Terms constitute the entire agreement between the Embassy and the Holder with respect to the Programme. No prior conversations, orbital alignments, encouraging glances, or messages sent at 2am constitute binding commitments unless confirmed in writing by the Ambassador. The Ambassador confirms very few things in writing. This is a feature of the Programme, not an oversight.

13. Contact

Correspondence with the Embassy is conducted via diplomatic channels, as established by the Ambassador. Unsolicited communications from non-Holders are received and not acknowledged. Communications from the current Holder are received and acknowledged with interest.
